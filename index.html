All You Need to Know About Pomskies

What is a Pomsky Anyway?

Pomskies are a new dog breed, they are the cross of a Siberian Husky and a Pomeranian.

Selective Dog Breeding

Selective dog breeding is the process in which we breed the dogs to selectively develop a particular trait. To develop the beloved Pomsky, breeders resorted to crosbreeding, which is the mix of two purebreds.

Characteristics and Traits

Since the breed is still very new, it's hard to predict the exact characteristics and traits the puppy will have. Some of the possible characteristics are to be small-medium in size, weight from 20-30 pounds, a beautiful silky coat, medium-length coat that is usually composed of two colors. Eye color can range from brown to blue, some even having different colored eyes. The Pomsky, being a dog on the smaller scale, is fit to be an apartment dog, though it requires moderate to high activity. Behaviourwise, we can expect the Pomsky to possess mixed traits of the Husky and Pomeranian.

Health of A Pomsky

There is not much information on their health due to being a new breed, but some of the health concerns are a mix of both Husky and Pomeranian health issues. Some of the health issues that are a major concern for a Pomsky are:

  • Patellar Luxation
  • Allergies
  • Eye Problems
  • Dental Problems

Minor concers:

  • Canine Hip Dysplasia
  • Collapsed Trachea
  • Skin Conditions

Why Train Your Pomsky

Pomskies can be stubborn and strong willed, thus it is ideal to train them as soon as possible to avoid any conflict with their behavior with other humans and dogs. Here we will list 5 essential things a Pomsky owner must teach their pup:

  1. Crate Training
  2. Biting Prevention
  3. Whining and Howling
  4. Pulling on the Leash
  5. Teaching Obedience
